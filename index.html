“Promoting policy and practice that improve the lives of vulnerable and abused adults in California”

CEJC is the only statewide multidisciplinary membership network devoted to elder justice. Our aim is to:

  • Provide a voice from the field to guide elder justice policy development
  • Create the critical mass that’s needed to elevate elder justice into the consciousness of policy makers
  • Provide policy makers, advocates, and stakeholders with streamlined, nonpartisan and interdisciplinary information
  • Explore problems and promising solutions
  • Provide opportunities for information sharing and consensus building

CEJC is a project of Community Partners

State to Fund APS Leader

Together with our partners, the California Commission on Aging and the County Welfare Directors Association, and in collaboration with Assemblymember Mariko Yamada, CEJC helped secure a state budget augmentation to support a full-time, dedicated Adult Protective Services (APS) position at the California Department of Social Services to oversee planning and administration of California’s APS program, provide leadership to county programs, coordinate with state and federal agencies, and oversee APS training.

Making California a safe, secure, and just place to grow old

We know the problems. We have ideas for solutions. But what California’s EJ network lacks is a united voice and opportunities to engage in collaborative, multidiscisciplinary problem-solving and policy development. CEJC was formed to harness the enormous expertise, talent, and passion that exists in California to assist policy makers formulate in-depth public policy that empowers and protects older Californians and ensures justice and accountability.
